## A confession

I am a frontend developer, but if you went through my browser history you would find — in order — a Rothko exhibition
review, a paper on rogue planets, a Dostoevsky audiobook, and the React documentation. Strictly in that order.

For a long time I thought this was a problem. The internet keeps telling us to **specialize**, to **niche down**, to
build a personal brand around a single keyword. I tried. It made me miserable.

### Curiosity compounds

What I've discovered is that the time I spend "off-topic" is the time that makes my actual work distinctive.

- Reading novels taught me **pacing**: how to hold a reader's attention across a long landing page.
- Looking at paintings taught me **composition**: how to balance a hero with negative space.
- Following space science taught me **scale**: how small even our biggest features really are.

If you're worried that your interests are too scattered to be useful — they aren't. They're the only thing that
makes your work yours.
